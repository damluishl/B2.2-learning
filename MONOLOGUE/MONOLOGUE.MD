# Monologue: The Holiday from Hell

"Well, they say that holidays are meant to be relaxing, but to be perfectly honest, my last trip to the coast was an absolute nightmare. It all started the very first day. I had been looking forward to the beach for months, but little did I know that my fair skin wouldn't handle the sun so well. I ended up with terrible sunburn all over my back. It was so painful that I had to lie down in the hotel room for the entire afternoon.

As if that wasn't enough, the next morning I woke up with a nasty cough and a sore throat. It hurt to swallow so much that I couldn't even enjoy the hotel breakfast. I thought I was just running a temperature, so I took some painkillers and hoped for the best. However, things quickly went from bad to worse.

That evening, we went to a local seafood restaurant. I must have eaten something off because, a few hours later, I started to feel really sick. I had a splitting headache and felt extremely dizzy—actually, 'light-headed' is a better word for it. Before I knew it, I had to run to the bathroom to throw up. Yes, it was classic food poisoning. I spent the rest of the night dealing with diarrhoea and wondering why I had ever left home!

The 'cherry on top' happened the last day. I tried to go for a walk to get some fresh air, but my new sandals were rubbing my heels, and I got a massive blister. While trying to avoid the pain, I tripped on a step and twisted my ankle. It became badly swollen almost immediately. A kind stranger helped me, but it was so hot outside that I almost passed out. Luckily, I came round quickly after someone gave me some water.

In the end, I had to go to the local clinic. They put on a bandage, gave me some antibiotics for my throat, and told me it would take a few weeks to get over everything. Looking back, next year I think I’ll just stay at home and keep a plaster and some antiseptic cream nearby, just in case!"
